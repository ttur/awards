Worldreader

Creating a Literate World.

Overview

Worldreader Mobile, a web-based reading solution for mobile phones built for Worldreader by Bitville and Futurice, enables millions of readers world-wide to access thousands of free books. The goal was to build a link between Worldreader’s online books and the 350 million people using Opera Mini.

Whether a beginner just learning to read or an adult pursuing a new direction in skills or education, life-long learning is now made more possible with this simple-to-use, platform-agnostic reading solution, available to anyone with an internet-connected phone. Millions of people use this new channel to visit and read, every month. And with features like Read for Children, which coaches parents on how to read to their children for best effect, impacts on literacy rates worldwide are certain to be positively affected.

User interface

Bitville had optimized user interfaces for Opera before, and so had the skills and knowledge to design and optimize user experiences for the Opera browser in this case. Bitville worked with Worldreader to create a cloud-based web app that makes books available so that even people with high latency, low-bandwidth connections can still enjoy reading on their phones.

Content management

The backend, built by Futurice, a content management system that helps the whole reading experience function smoothly. The system automatically analyzes and pre-processes book information, which is then edited further by the management team. Automatically generated data, such as title and author, is enriched with added categories and defined taxonomies. Books can also be featured in country-specific top lists and collections.

Content management system helps Worldreader editors build context aware selections that cater reader's needs in different parts of the world. This helps readers focus on their studies, favorite subjects, or particular authors.

Mobile reading is convenient and inexpensive, even free. One-third of mobile readers use their mobile phones to read stories to children, thereby supporting literacy acquisition and other forms of learning.
62% of people who disliked reading like it more when they begin reading on a mobile phone, while 66% of those who liked reading to begin with like it even more.
62% of users read more now that they use their mobile for reading.
